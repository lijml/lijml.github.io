    •     Honoring Supervisor Mark Ridley-Thomas  

          Los Angeles, January 29, 2009 --    The Los Angeles County Asian American Employees Association (LACAAEA) hosts a special reception honoring supervisor Mark Ridley-Thomas at Empress Pavilion Restaurant, Los Angeles, California, on Thursday evening, January 29, 2009.

    This special Meet & Greet reception falls on the first week of the the Chinese Lunar New Year of Ox, known as Spring Festival, the most important festival for family reunions in China with a population of 1.3 billion. According to Chinese Zodiac, the Year of 2009 is a Year of the Ox which lasts from January 26, 2009 to February 14, 2010. The Chinese New Year does not begin on 1st of January, but on a date that corresponds with the second New Moon after the winter equinox, so it varies from year to year.

    The whole evening event basks in a joyful festive atmosphere as nearly 300 attendees honor Supervisor Mark Ridley-Thomas along with many dignitaries and nearly 20 Los Angeles County Department Heads, with splendid and colorful folk and lion dance performed by Khmer Arts Academy (Blessing Dance), IMAGE Pinoy Dance Troupe of Los Angeles, and East Wind Lion Dance Troupe, while millions of Chinese people around the world celebrating the Lunar New Year of Ox. William T Fujioka wishes every attendee a Happy New Year at the end of his special welcome remark.
