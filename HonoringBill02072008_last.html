LACAAEA Honoring William T Fujioka on Feb. 7, 2008
 Honoring His Appointment To Los Angeles County CEO

        Los Angeles, Feb. 7, 2008 -- The LACAAEA Meet and Greet Reception honoring William T Fujioka's appointment as Chief Executive Officer for the County of Los Angles was held on Thursday, Feb. 7, 2008 at Ocean Star Seafood Restaurant in Monterey Park, California, where lanterns and streamers were decorated as the Chinese Lunar New Year of the Rat, known as Spring Festival, the most important festival for family reunions in China with a population of 1.3 billion, falls on Feb. 7 this year. The rat is the smartest animal among the 12 zodiac signs in Chinese traditional culture and is a symbol of prosperity.

       A famous traditional Lion Dance, a form of traditional dance in Chinese culture, in which performers mimic a lion's movements in a lion costume, was performed during the reception. The dance was accompanied by gongs, drums, representing the descent of good luck. Both lion dancers eat lettuce hanging from a pole William T Fujioka holds for good luck during the performance. This yields a fantastic evening reception "Honoring William T Fujioka" to more than 300 audiences.

       The audience was comprised of Superior Court Judges, County Department Heads (Cynthia Banks, Janice Y Fukai, Steven Golightly, Dave Lambertson, Dean C. Logan, Marcia Mayeda, Tyler McCauley, Dennis A. Tafoya and Margaret Todd), many LACAAEA present, past, and retired members, Los Angeles County Cambodian Employee Association, Los Angeles County Filipino Association and Special Guest, Vice Chair, State Board of Equalization Judy Chu, who presented a certificate to William T Fujioka. (Click here to view a slide show! )

       Janice Y Fukai, ALTERNATE PUBLIC DEFENDER, a recipient of the Los Angeles County’s Quality and Productivity Commission’s “2007 Chair Leadership Award” and Los Angeles County Bar Association’s “Defense Attorney of the Year Award" served as the mistress of ceremonies for this historic event. LACAAEA would like to thank Janice for maintaining the event pace and keeping the program on track. We are grateful for her time and efforts during the evening. She is also a long time LACAAEA member and supporter.

    Shig Kikkawa, former LACAAEA president and colleague of William T Fujioka, introduced our guest of honor. Mr. Kikkawa’s introduction was heartfelt and inspiring. First, Mr. Kikkawa emphasized the significance that our County’s first Chief Executive Officer is an Asian American, stating, “We would have never thought this could be possible”. Mr. Kikkawa noted several other successful Asian American County Department Heads, who helped pave the way for Mr. Fujioka’s historic appointment to the highest post in Los Angeles County. He also stressed the importance that we work together for new “Chief” to ensure his continued success and to the benefit of us all.

    William T Fujioka, our Chief Executive Officer and Guest of Honor, was self assured, humorous and sincere. He thanked everyone for attending the event and gave special thanks to the Department Heads, who he noted had already attended numerous receptions in his honor in recent past. It was clear from the many Department Heads present, that Mr. Fujioka had already gained their respect and support.

    Mr Fujioka explained his reasons for coming out of retirement from Los Angeles City, to accept the top position for the County. (Mr. Fujioka had been retired after successfully serving as the first Asian American Chief Administrative Officer for Los Angeles City). “I returned to the County because I love public service” he said, and reminded everyone in the audience of the number of lives that could be helped by each of us doing our county jobs... “Just think”, he reminded us, “how just one phone call could change a person’s life”.

    Mr. Fujioka also described what he enjoyed most about his new position: That he did not have to take the job for the money, and as such, he did not have to compromise his values, beliefs and principles in order to “keep his job”. He explained how he respectfully serves five Board of Supervisors, and as long as he maintains their trust and support, he could and would do what he believed was in the very best interests of the residents of Los Angeles County. A huge and enthusiastic round of applause from the excited crowd followed.

    We also thank Mr. Fujioka, for honoring us with the presence of his family: Wife Darlene Kuba; Brother Honorable Fred Fujioka, Judge of the Superior Court and Fred’s wife Jane Fujioka; Family Friends; and Mother Linda Fujioka. Mr. Fujioka acknowledged each of his family members and gave special tribute to his mother Linda, who he credited for his successes and achievements. It was a heartwarming moment and revealed the importance of family to our new “Chief”.

    Howard Takata, who Janice described as the “godfather of LACAAEA”, closed the evening with a congratulations toast. It was obvious to all, how exceptionally proud Howard is of “Bill”’s appointment. He advised “Bill”, to first and foremost, take care of his health and family (so true). Howard then urged the audience, to support our “Chief” with all our hearts. As we raised our glasses with Howard, we pledged to Mr. Fujioka, our warmest congratulations and support.

    We hope that you had the opportunity to personally meet William T Fujioka at the reception and enjoyed the great food, entertainment and inspirational speeches. We thank everyone in attendance for helping us welcome back our new Chief Executive Officer, WILLIAM T FUJIOKA. And, we wish you and your family a very Happy, Healthy, and Prosperous New Year - "Gung Hay Fat Choy"!
