LACAAEA's 28th Annual Fish Fry and Annual Scholarship Awards, Sept. 17, 2011
 LACAAEA'S 28th Annual Fish Fry
   The Los Angeles County Asian American Employees Association (LACAAEA) hosted the 28th Annual Fish Fry 2011 on Saturday, September 17, 2011 at Marina del Rey, California.
   The Los Angeles County Asian American Employees Association (LACAAEA) would like to thank the Los Angeles County Board of Supervisors' support for this event.

LACAAEA Annual Scholarship Awards
LACAAEA wishes each of the 2011 scholarship recipients the best in both their academic and professional careers. We are proud to be able to support these talented young people and wish them the very best. Here's the short speech from Greg Sasaki, Scholarship Committee Chairman.
   Tiffany Lue received the Shirley Bolinger Four-Year Undergraduate Scholarship award.
   Jonathan Trinh, class of 2015 at UCLA (The second best public university in the nation according to the 2012 edition of U.S. News & World Report), received the Shirley Bolinger Four-Year Undergraduate Scholarship award.
   Lori Li, class of 2014 at USC Marshall School, received the Ed Lee Continuing Undergraduate Scholarship award.

    The 28th annual Fish Fry was held on Saturday, September 17, 2011 at Mother’s Beach in Marina del Rey. The lingering marina layer helped keep the morning temperatures at a comfortable level while many LACAAEA members and Board officers arrived early to help with the set up and food preparations. A number of these early arrivals ventured out for a walk to the Venice canals (Click here for a photo) and back. The first ever Fish Fry 5k Walk was led by Paul Wong. The day featured bright sunshine that burned through the marina layer shortly before noon and continued throughout the day. The beautiful California weather contributed to a very festive environment. Overall, nearly 150 people--LACAAEA members, family, and friends--enjoyed a great day at Mother’s Beach!

    Former LACAAEA Presidents and County retirees Henry Yee and Phil Ow relinquished their lengthy roles as the main chefs at a Fish Fry. Instead, they provided guidance and passed on the cooking baton to Kenji Mayeda, Karen Tu, Adrienne Ng, Susie Yan, Borina Nou, Mark Limon, and others who helped prepare a magnificent meal.

    Ed Jewik, Richard Jewik, Vicky Gutierrez, Connie Diep and Darlene Hoang helped serve the food. Dulce Maycumber shopped for the many raffles prizes and other items handed out to eager attendees. The registration table was covered by Sam Men, Jung Son, and Linda Quon. The set-up crew included Danny Young, Wayne Watts, George Yan, Mary Lam, Judy Wong, Khrystyne Fong, Cannice Chen, and Konthea Kang. Susan Kudo-Lee was in charge of the crafts table, while Lorraine Sunday-Brown brought the music, DJ equipment, and oversaw the kids’ relay races. Isaura Capell prepared several pictures boards that featured the 2011 Asian Pacific American Heritage Day celebration and Officers’ Installation Dinner. A number of organizations were also in attendance to provide information to those present: Asian Pacific Community Fund (APCF), Local SEIU, Asian Youth Center (AYC), Robert Half International, and State Farm.

   The LACAAEA Annual Scholarship Awards were once again presented at a Fish Fry. The LACAAEA wishes each of the 2011 scholarship recipients the best in both their academic and professional careers. We are proud to be able to support these talented young people and wish them the very best. Greg Sasaki, Scholarship Committee Chairman, introduced each of the recipients: Tiffany Lue and Jonathan Trinh were both awarded Shirley Bolinger Scholarships, while Lori Li, class of 2014 at USC’s Marshall School of Business, received the Ed Lee Continuing Undergraduate Scholarship award.

The Fish Fry Planning Committee consisted of: Isaura Capell, Mason Fong, Armando Garcia, Jackie Guevarra, Ed Jewik, Susan Kudo-Lee, Mary Lam, Jeremy Li, Sam Men Limon, Dulce Maycumber, Kenji Mayeda, Adrienne Ng, Borina Nou, Phil Ow, Marc Limon, Greg Sasaki, Tevy Sithan, Karen Tu, Lorraine Sunday-Brown, Paul Wong, George Yan, Susie Yan, Danny Young, and Henry Yee.

The Fish Fry has served, for many years, as a mechanism by the Association to thank our members for their membership, constant support, and loyalty. The LACAAEA is grateful to all of you who volunteered to help make the Fish Fry a successful event once again. Without your efforts, the Fish Fry would not have been a reality! We also want to thank everyone who attended the event. Your presence helped enhance the Fish Fry! The LACAAEA would also like to thank the Board of Supervisors' for their support of this event.
