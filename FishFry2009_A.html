The 26th Annual Fish Fry on October 3, 2009
 Fish Fry, 2009
  •    The Los Angeles County Asian American Employees Association (LACAAEA) hosts the 26th Annual Fish Fry 2009 on Saturday, October 3, 2009 at Marina del Rey, California.
  •    Over 120 AAEA members and their family members attended the event.
  •    The Los Angeles County Asian American Employees Association (LACAAEA) would like to thank the Board of Supervisors' support for this event.
  •    Annual Scholarship Presentation
    • Photo Gallery The 25th Fish Fry, 2008


        Los Angeles, Oct. 3, 2009 -- The 26th annual "Fish Fry" was held on Saturday, October 3, 2009, bringing together members of LACAAEA, their families and friends at Mother's Beach, Marina del Rey, California. It was an exceptionally beautiful fall day with clear blue skies and bright sunshine with little wind. Coincidently, the traditional Chinese Mid-Autumn Festival, also known as the Moon Festival, falls on October 3, 2009 this year, the fifteenth day of the eighth lunar month on the lunar calendar. Therefore, some members of LACAAEA enjoyed the moon cakes at the Fish Fry festival in the earlier morning to observe this traditional festival that boasts a history of thousands of years. The luminous full moon is always faithful to be there when Mid-Autumn Day falls.

        Henry Yee and Phillip Ow once again prepared delicious meals for everyone in attendance. As always, Phillip cooked with fried fish, steamed fish, and fried hot dogs while Henry stewed up his signature seafood dish. LACAAEA officers and members set up shop at 7:00 a.m., while Henry and Phillip prepared some delicious fried rice for these early risers. Lorraine and Evan Sunday-Brown provided the music and a few games such as water balloons (Click here for photos).

       Members and their families started to arrive and join on the annual festival around 10:30 a.m., and brought some dishes and desert for the event. As always, Lorraine and Evan put together some really fun games for both kids and parents to enjoy! There was also bingo and raffles hosted by Irene Hamada, and Jackie Guevarra.

    The Fish Fry festival is a great opportunity for the networking and see old friends, meet new ones, relax, enjoy the ocean air and sun! If you were unable to attend, we hope you can visit LACAAEA website to view photos and hope you will not miss the event next year.
