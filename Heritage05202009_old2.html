    •       Los Angeles, May 20, 2009 --     The Los Angeles County Asian American Employees Association (LACAAEA) organized the wonderful festivities to celebrate and recognize the countless contributions and accomplishments of Asian and Pacific Islanders in Los Angeles County. The celebration was held at the Kenneth Hahn Hall of Administration Mall, 500 West Temple Street, Los Angeles, California.

    Leyna Nguyen, Anchor from KCBS 2 and KCAL 9 served as our illustrious Mistress of Ceremonies. We are extremely grateful that Ms. Nguyen could share her own personal boat experience, immigration to this Country, and how she was given the opportunity to pursue her dreams. Ms. Nguyen is a major news Anchor woman in Los Angeles who previously supported our Association. Thank you again, Ms. Nguyen for your continued commitment, dedication, and support.

    Los Angeles County Supervisors Don Knabe, Fourth District; Michael D. Antonovich, Fifth District; and Mark Ridley-Thomas, Second District all spoke about the importance of the Asian Pacific American Heritage Month and wished everyone well.

    William T. Fujioka, the Chief Executive Officer of Los Angeles County, spoke about his own personal background, heritage, and how the American system of government has allowed him the opportunity to ascend to one of the highest position in Los Angeles County

    Paul K. Tanaka, Assistant Sheriff of Los Angeles County, Mayor for the City of Gardena, as the Keynote Speaker, addressed the tough economic conditions facing the nation and the world.  He encouraged everyone to say “Thank You” to each of the County Supervisors for their jobs and the tremendous effort and commitment each Supervisor makes every day.  A quote “You do not have to say your name, just say “Thank you, Supervisor, when you see any supervisor next time."

    Christine Chiang, Anchor/Reporter, LA 18/KSCI TV and supporter of the Asian Pacific Community Fund (APCF) received the Outstanding Service Award. The award was presented by City of Gardena Councilman Ronald Ikejiri to Ms. Chiang. Ms. Chiang recognized Wendy Watanabe, Auditor-Controller of Los Angeles County for her support and help in making this award possible.

    The Los Angeles County Asian American Employees Association would like to thank everyone who supported this wonderful event. The Association would also like to take this opportunity to thank the Board of Supervisors for their continued support of Asian Pacific American Heritage Month. Your commitment and support is always truly appreciated. Thank you Supervisors!
