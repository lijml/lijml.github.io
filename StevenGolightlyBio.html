Steven J. Golightly, Child Support Services Department

Steven Golightly,  Director of the Los Angeles County Child Support Services Department.
More photos >>
     Steven J. Golightly currently serves as Director of the Los Angeles County Child Support Services Department, the largest locally-administered child support program in the nation. He was appointed to this position by the Board of Supervisors on February 23, 2007. As Director, he currently oversees the day-to-day operations of the County’s Child Support agency with an annual budget of $185 million and a workforce of over 1,800 individuals.

Prior to joining the County, Mr. Golightly worked with the National Alliance of Business (NAB), in Washington DC, where he worked as Vice President for Human Service Programs, was a career member of the Federal government’s Senior Executive Services, and served as Regional Hub Director and Regional Administrator for the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. He also served as Chief Deputy Director and Acting Director at the Los Angeles County Department of Public Social Services.

He has an undergraduate degree in Business Management and a Masters Degree in Public Administration. He will begin his Ph.D. studies in Public Policy and Administration in December 2007.

Introduction by: Anthony Kim, Supervising Child Support Officer Child Support Services Department

Anthony Kim started his career with the Child Support Services Department in 1997 as a Child Support Officer assigned to the Call Center Division. In December 2000, he was promoted to the position of Supervising Child Support Officer and was later reassigned to the Branch Operations Unit in Division 4. Furthermore, as a good citizen, in July 2006, Mr. Kim was appointed by the Long Beach City Mayor, confirmed by the City Council members, to serve as a Commissioner over the Community Development Advisory Commission; he currently serves on the Budget and Neighborhood Assistance Program Committees.
